# An Autistic Student in Your Residence / Student-Health Team

> **For residence-life, halls & student-health nursing staff** — supporting an autistic **student** (roughly ages 18–25).

## The one idea {.lead}
Autism, through **monotropism**, means a student's attention locks into a few deep, intense "tunnels." Halls of residence and student-health settings — shared, noisy, unpredictable, full of new demands — are among the hardest environments a young autistic adult enters, and many **hit a wall** (burnout, anxiety, crisis) in their first year. You may be the first safe professional they meet. Predictable, low-sensory, literal care and a quick route to the right support can be genuinely life-changing.

## What you may see — and what's really going on

| You may see… | What's often happening |
|---|---|
| Self-neglect, not eating/sleeping, room-bound | **Autistic burnout** from cumulative university load |
| Avoids the shared kitchen, the social floor, the surgery | Sensory/social barriers — not laziness or "adjusting" |
| Doesn't report illness or pain until severe | **Interoception** differences; "not saying" ≠ "not suffering" |
| Goes silent, withdraws for days | Shutdown/recovery under overwhelm |
| First identified only now, in crisis | A common late-identification point |

## Try this {.do}
- **Be predictable and low-sensory** — a quiet room, a named contact, written information, advance warning.
- **Offer written/online options** for appointments; allow a supporter and a sensory kit.
- **Ask proactively about mental health, sleep, eating and safety** — literally and separately.
- **Know the signposts** — disability service, wellbeing, mentoring, crisis lines — and go *with* them.
- **Treat a crisis as unmet need**, not "can't cope with independence"; reduced load is a legitimate step.

## Avoid this {.dont}
- Bright, noisy, surprise-led assessments; vague or verbal-only information.
- Equating silence, self-neglect or a flat affect with "choosing not to engage."
- Defaulting to "typical student stress" and missing burnout or a first identification.

## When to get more support {.support}
> Watch for **autistic burnout** (exhaustion, loss of skills, reduced tolerance) and **suicidality** — screen and ask directly. Signpost the disability/wellbeing service, mentoring and autistic student communities. A temporary reduction in load or interruption of studies is an evidence-aligned recovery step.

## If the student is a woman {.gender}
> Late-identified women mask intensely; a "sociable high-achiever" may be privately collapsing. Take withdrawal and reduced tolerance seriously, however together they look.
