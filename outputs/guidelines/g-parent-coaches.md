# An Autistic Parent in Your Activity / Group

> **For activity, toddler-group & family-class leaders** — supporting an autistic **parent** who joins with (or alongside) their child.

## The one idea {.lead}
Autism, through **monotropism**, means a parent's attention locks into a few deep, intense "tunnels." Parent-and-child groups can be a sensory and social minefield — chatter, singing, last-minute changes, the pressure to perform "warmly" — on top of an already depleted parent. A few predictable, low-demand adjustments help an autistic parent attend, enjoy and connect with their child, and may be the difference between them isolating at home and building a support network.

## What you may see — and what's really going on

| You may see… | What's often happening |
|---|---|
| Arrives late/early to avoid the crowd; sits at the edge | Managing sensory/social load — not unfriendly |
| Wants the routine, the plan, the same spot | Predictability lowers the load |
| Goes quiet or leaves suddenly | Overload or shutdown — involuntary, not rude |
| Intensely engaged in one activity with their child | **Monotropic focus** — a strength, not "narrow" |
| Overwhelmed by singing, chatter, "share with the group" | High-channel demand on a depleted system |

## Try this {.do}
- **Share the plan in advance** — the order of activities, what to bring, how it ends; keep it consistent week to week.
- **Lower the sensory load** — a quieter corner, less forced singing/noise, a clear start and end.
- **Offer low-pressure ways to join** — a job (handing out, tidying), side-by-side over face-to-face chat.
- **Welcome stimming, headphones and a supporter**, and a literal, no-surprises style.
- **Build the relationship** — a leader who "gets it" makes the group safe enough to return to.

## Avoid this {.dont}
- Surprise changes, "everyone introduce yourselves" pressure, or being put on the spot.
- Reading a quiet or sudden exit as rudeness or disinterest.
- Assuming a "flat" affect means they're not enjoying it.

## When to get more support {.support}
> Recognise the high **burnout load** many autistic parents carry (often while raising a neurodivergent child). Signpost autism-led parent support and keep attendance flexible — a parent who can drop in and out without judgement is far more likely to stay connected.

## If the parent is a mother {.gender}
> Autistic mothers mask intensely and are often misdiagnosed; a "coping" surface can hide real overload. Take a literal, low-demand approach regardless of how together they look.
