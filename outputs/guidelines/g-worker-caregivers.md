# An Autistic Worker You Support / Coach

> **For job coaches, employment-support workers & workplace mentors** — supporting an autistic **adult in employment**.

## The one idea {.lead}
Autism, through **monotropism**, means an adult's attention locks into a few deep, intense "tunnels." In the right role that's a superpower — depth, reliability, pattern-detection, honesty. In the wrong environment (open-plan, constant interruption, unclear expectations) it drives masking and **autistic burnout**. As a job coach or support worker you're often the bridge between a capable worker and a workplace that wasn't designed for them: help articulate adjustments, protect focus, and read the load before burnout sets in.

## What you may see — and what's really going on

| You may see… | What's often happening |
|---|---|
| Exhausted, losing skills, can't recover after work | **Autistic burnout** — distinct from occupational burnout and depression |
| "Fine" at work, collapses afterwards | Heavy **masking**; the cost is paid out of sight |
| Struggles with open-plan, meetings, hot-desking | High-channel, unpredictable environments tax a monotropic system |
| Won't report pain, side-effects or overload | **Interoception** differences; "not saying" ≠ "not suffering" |
| Wants everything in writing; avoids calls | Single-channel communication reduces the load |

## Try this {.do}
- **Help articulate reasonable adjustments** (Equality Act / ADA): quiet workspace, written instructions, fewer meetings, asynchronous communication, clear expectations.
- **Structure the role around fewer, deeper "tunnels, not tasks"** — minimise interruptions, batch shallow work, protect flow.
- **Be the predictable, safe check-in** — literal, written where helpful, focused on load and recovery.
- **Take burnout seriously** — if it's brewing, the evidence-backed move is reduce demands and allow unmasking, not "push through."
- **Centre their expertise** — they know what helps; help them ask for it.

## Avoid this {.dont}
- Generic "social-skills" or "push through" advice that increases masking.
- Equating a masked, competent performance with being "fine."
- Speaking *for* them without involving them in decisions.

## When to get more support {.support}
> Watch for **autistic burnout** and **suicidality** — burnout is linked to it. Address co-occurring **ADHD (AuDHD), anxiety, depression, sleep and GI conditions**. Where burnout is severe, validate time off, reduced hours or a role change as recovery. Signpost Access to Work / occupational health.

## If the worker is a woman {.gender}
> Late-diagnosed women mask heavily and may look "fine" while privately exhausted; take a lifelong pattern of difference and overload seriously.
