# An Autistic Parent in Your Nursing / Maternity Team

> **For midwives, health visitors, maternity & community-nursing staff** — supporting an autistic **adult who is (or is becoming) a parent**.

## The one idea {.lead}
Autism, through **monotropism**, means a parent's attention locks into a few deep, intense "tunnels." Pregnancy, birth and early parenting are relentless, high-channel and unpredictable — exactly the conditions a monotropic system finds hardest — so autistic parents carry a **high risk of burnout** and often ignore their own needs. Maternity and community-nursing settings can be sensory-hostile and surprise-heavy. Predictable, calm, literal care and proactive checking protect both parent and baby.

## What you may see — and what's really going on

| You may see… | What's often happening |
|---|---|
| Sensory-overloaded, anxious or shut down in clinic/labour | A high-channel environment on a monotropic system — not "not coping" |
| Skips appointments, ignores own pain or symptoms | **Interoception** differences + hyper-focus on the baby |
| Rigid routines around feeding and settling | Predictability reduces the load — not "controlling" |
| Diagnosed only after their child is | A common route to adult identification |
| Goes quiet or non-verbal under pressure | Shutdown — needs calm, not pressure to "engage" |

## Try this {.do}
- **Offer the AASPIRE *AHAT*** and **written-first** options; allow a partner or supporter.
- **Make perinatal care predictable**: same clinician, calm room, clear written plan; explain each step before doing it.
- **Ask proactively about pain, exhaustion and eating** — they will under-report.
- **Lower the sensory load** in clinic and on the ward (dim light, fewer beeps, fewer surprises).
- **Screen for autistic burnout and perinatal mental-illness** — the two overlap and both need specific care.

## Avoid this {.dont}
- Misreading overload or shutdown as "not bonding" with the baby, or as depression alone.
- Surprise home visits without warning, or unpredictable procedures.
- Assuming they'll self-refer when struggling — they often won't.

## When to get more support {.support}
> Watch for **autistic burnout** and perinatal mental-illness; address co-occurring **ADHD, anxiety, sleep and GI conditions**. Signpost **autistic-parent peer support** and practical help. Where the household includes a neurodivergent child, coordinate with paediatric services.

## If the parent is a mother {.gender}
> Autistic mothers are frequently misdiagnosed (perinatal anxiety/depression) and mask intensely, including with clinicians. Ask about lifelong social/sensory differences, not just current mood.
