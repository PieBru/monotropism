# Your Autistic Fellow Student / Friend

> **For fellow students, flatmates & friends** — getting along with an autistic **university student** (roughly ages 18–25).

## The one idea {.lead}
Autism, through **monotropism**, means a friend's attention locks into a few deep, intense "tunnels." University is a *massive* transition, and many autistic students **hit a wall** — burnout, anxiety or crisis — in their first year, often while looking "fine" on the surface. They might prefer text over calls, need a predictable flat, avoid loud socials, and go quiet when overwhelmed. Being the flatmate or friend who keeps things calm, says what they mean, and doesn't take a withdrawal personally can be the anchor that keeps them here.

## What you may see — and what's really going on

| You may see… | What's often happening |
|---|---|
| Texts not calls; avoids the group-chat drama | Single-channel communication reduces the load |
| Goes quiet or room-bound for days | Shutdown/recovery under overwhelm — not ghosting you |
| Intense focus on one subject; skips "small talk" | A **passion** and a strength — connect there |
| Hates loud flats, surprise guests, last-minute plans | Sensory/social overload; predictability is relief |
| Takes things literally; bluntly honest | Literal communication — usually well-meant |

## Try this {.do}
- **Agree flat norms early and in writing** — noise, guests, shared space; predictability helps everyone.
- **Default to text**, give notice before guests or changes, and protect quiet time.
- **Connect through the interest** — it's where they're most themselves.
- **Check in literally and privately** if they go quiet ("you okay? need anything?").
- **Know who to call** — wellbeing service, mentor, crisis line — and offer to go with them.

## Avoid this {.dont}
- Surprise guests, loud late nights, or "spontaneous" demands.
- Taking a withdrawal or a blunt text personally.
- Pushing them into loud socials or "you need to get out more."

## When to get more support {.support}
> If your friend seems to be burning out (exhausted, losing skills, withdrawing) or talks about hopelessness, **don't keep it to yourself** — help them reach the wellbeing/disability service or a crisis line. One steady friend makes a real difference.

## If your friend is a woman {.gender}
> Late-identified women mask intensely and may look like a "sociable high-achiever" while privately collapsing; take withdrawal and reduced tolerance seriously, however together they seem.
