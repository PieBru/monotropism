# An Autistic Parent You Support

> **For doulas, PAs, support workers & family aides** — supporting an autistic **adult who is (or is becoming) a parent**.

## The one idea {.lead}
Autism, through **monotropism**, means a parent's attention locks into a few deep, intense "tunnels." Parenting is relentless, high-channel and unpredictable — exactly the conditions a monotropic system finds hardest — so autistic parents carry a **high risk of burnout** and often ignore their own needs. As a support worker you can be the calm, predictable, practical presence that keeps them and their family afloat: follow their routines, lower the load, and watch for the burnout they'll under-report.

## What you may see — and what's really going on

| You may see… | What's often happening |
|---|---|
| Devoted, yet sensory-overloaded and "can't cope" | Parenting load on a monotropic system — high burnout risk |
| Skips meals, ignores own pain or exhaustion | **Interoception** differences + hyper-focus on the child |
| Anxious, rigid routines around the baby | Predictability reduces the load — not "controlling" |
| Goes quiet or shuts down | Overload/shutdown — needs calm, not pressure |
| Wants the same plan, the same help, every time | Consistency lowers a sky-high load |

## Try this {.do}
- **Follow their routines and preferences** — consistency across visits lowers the load; never undo their system "your" way.
- **Be predictable** — same person, same time, advance notice of any change; warn before visits.
- **Offer concrete, specific help** — a meal, a sleep window, a tidy, a feed — and protect their interest/recovery time.
- **Communicate literally and in writing** where it helps; allow processing time and choice.
- **Watch for burnout** and ask proactively about pain, exhaustion and eating — they will under-report.

## Avoid this {.dont}
- Surprise visits, sudden changes, or taking over their routines.
- Misreading overload or shutdown as "not bonding" or "not coping."
- Adding social or sensory demands on an already depleted parent.

## When to get more support {.support}
> Watch for **autistic burnout** and perinatal mental-illness (the two overlap and both need specific care). Signpost **autistic-parent peer support** and escalate to the GP/health visitor if you see crisis signs. Where a child is also neurodivergent, recognise the compounding load.

## If the parent is a mother {.gender}
> Autistic mothers are frequently misdiagnosed and mask intensely, including with support staff. Ask about lifelong social/sensory differences, not just current mood.
