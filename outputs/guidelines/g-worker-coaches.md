# An Autistic Adult in Your Coaching / Gym / Club

> **For gym instructors, running-club leaders & sports coaches** — supporting an autistic **adult**.

## The one idea {.lead}
Autism, through **monotropism**, means an adult's attention locks into a few deep, intense "tunnels." For a working autistic adult, your gym, class or club can be essential recovery — a place of routine, focus and regulation after a high-load day at work. Many will mask all day and need the session to be low-demand and predictable, not another performance. Lean into structure, clarity and quiet, and your coaching becomes genuine wellbeing support.

## What you may see — and what's really going on

| You may see… | What's often happening |
|---|---|
| Same time, same machine, same routine, every visit | Predictability and flow are **regulation**, not "rigidity" |
| Wants the plan in writing; hates ad-hoc changes | Single-channel, literal planning — gives control |
| Comes straight from work, depleted or "flat" | Likely end-of-day burnout/masking fatigue |
| Headphones in, avoids chat | Managing sensory and social load — not unfriendly |
| Masters technique deeply; asks detailed "why" questions | **Monotropic strength** and engagement |

## Try this {.do}
- **Offer written plans and predictable structure**; tell them what the session is before it starts.
- **One cue at a time, with reasons**; allow processing time; show the movement.
- **Respect headphones, quiet and routine** — these are regulation tools; don't push unsolicited chat.
- **Play to depth and interest** — technique, programming, a single discipline often suits.
- **Check in literally and briefly**; offer individual over forced group formats where possible.

## Avoid this {.dont}
- Surprise partner-work, "motivational" shouting, or last-minute class changes.
- Reading headphones or a quiet routine as rudeness or lack of effort.
- Equating a flat, depleted session with "not trying."

## When to get more support {.support}
> Be alert to **burnout signs** (chronic exhaustion, withdrawal, dropping skills) and signpost occupational health / GP support if welcome. A flexible, low-demand membership helps an autistic adult keep moving even through a hard patch at work.

## If the person is a woman {.gender}
> Late-diagnosed women mask heavily and may present as "fine" while privately exhausted; don't let a competent exterior override signs of overload.
