# Your Autistic Friend (a Parent)

> **For friends, school-gate peers & fellow parents** — being a good friend to an autistic **adult who is a parent**.

## The one idea {.lead}
Autism, through **monotropism**, means a friend's attention locks into a few deep, intense "tunnels." Add parenting and the load is sky-high, so autistic parents are often **exhausted, masked and isolated** — and may decline, cancel or go quiet far more than other friends. None of that means they don't value you; it means their budget is spent. Low-pressure, predictable, judgment-free friendship — concrete help, no guilt, connection through shared interest — is exactly the lifeline an autistic parent needs.

## What you may see — and what's really going on

| You may see… | What's often happening |
|---|---|
| Cancels, declines, or goes quiet for stretches | Overloaded and budgeting a finite supply — not about you |
| Texts not calls; avoids the school-gate chat | Single-channel communication reduces the load |
| Rigid routines; says "no" to surprises | Predictability is survival, not controlling |
| Blunt, literal, or "too honest" | Literal communication — usually well-meant |
| Intense about one interest; skips the small talk | A passion — connect there |

## Try this {.do}
- **Offer concrete help** — a meal, a school run, an hour of child-minding; specific beats "let me know."
- **Keep it low-pressure and flexible** — no guilt for cancellations; text over call.
- **Connect through a shared interest**, side-by-side over face-to-face.
- **Say what you mean**; assume good intent in blunt exchanges.
- **Check in literally and leave the door open** — "no need to reply."

## Avoid this {.dont}
- Guilt-trips, surprise visits, or "you never come to anything."
- Reading a cancellation or a blunt text as coldness or friendship-fading.
- Well-meant but exhausting advice about their parenting or routine.

## When to get more support {.support}
> Autistic parents carry a **high burnout load**, often while raising a neurodivergent child. If your friend seems overwhelmed or hopeless, gently signpost autism-led parent support and, if you're worried, help them reach their GP or health visitor. Being the non-judgmental friend is itself powerful support.

## If your friend is a mother {.gender}
> Autistic mothers mask intensely and are often misdiagnosed; a "coping" surface can hide real overload. Take what they *don't* say as seriously as what they do.
