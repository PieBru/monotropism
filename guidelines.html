🟢 English seed — fine-tuning in progress

The doctor / health-professional column and the primary-school row are complete first, as a representative vertical and horizontal slice. Once the template and content are fine-tuned, the remaining cells will be filled, and the whole grid will be translated into the library's other languages. Source for every sheet is tracked in the repository; all guides are AI-assisted and derived from the project's existing research drafts (the Monotropism brief, the Lifespan Practitioner & Health-Care guide, the Family guide, and Growing Up Monotropic), with local source archives used where needed.

🧭 How the grid is organised

Rows = the autistic (monotropic) person, by life stage. Columns = the reader — the counterpart supporting them. Gender (male / female) is handled as a short, cross-cutting callout inside each sheet (on camouflaging and the under-identified female presentation) rather than as a separate axis, so the grid stays an 8 × 8 — flag this if you'd prefer a separate axis.

⚖️ About these guides

Uses identity-first language (“autistic person”). Informational — not medical advice or a diagnostic tool. Monotropism is a powerful but still-developing theory; old-age and high-support evidence is thin. Always fit guidance to the individual in front of you. Read the full research drafts behind any sheet via the main library.
